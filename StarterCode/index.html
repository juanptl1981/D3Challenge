Correlations Discovered Between Obesity Rates and Poverty

In general, there appears to be an upward trend between obesity rates and poverty rates. Specifically, as poverty rates tend to rise, obesity rates tend to rise as well. This makes sense, given that unhealthy food tends to be much cheaper and easier to access than healthier items. However, there are some notable outliers: North Dakota appears to have a higher obesity rate despite having a lower poverty rate and Washington, D.C. appears to have a much lower obesity rate despite having a higher poverty rate.
